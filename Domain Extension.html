DOMAIN EXTENSIONS

CHOOSING THE RIGHT ONE

A domain extension is the suffix at the end of your web address, such as .com, .org, or .id. Each has its own purpose:

  • .com — Commercial or general use (most popular).
  • .org — For organizations or non-profits.
  • .edu — Educational institutions.
  • .id — Indonesian local websites.
  • .net — Networking or technical projects.

Choosing the right extension helps users immediately understand what kind of site they’re visiting and builds credibility.
